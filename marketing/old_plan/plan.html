One-page marketing plan (general sketch)

Objective

  • Primary: build a reliable loop of creator supply → quality content → discovery → retention → creator earnings.
  • Near-term: acquire the first cohort of creators and early adopters; validate which content formats and discovery surfaces drive repeat usage.

Target audiences (ICP)

Creators

  • indie teachers, writers, coaches, niche experts
  • value: easy publishing, distribution, community, monetization

Consumers / learners

  • people who like short, structured learning + discovery
  • value: trustworthy content, clear navigation, saved progress, recommendations

Core funnel

  • Awareness: SEO pages + social distribution + partner/community mentions.
  • Activation: landing pages that demonstrate value in 10–30 seconds (examples, previews, “follow creator”, “save”, “start”).
  • Retention: notifications/digests, “continue where you left off”, subscriptions, contests/events.
  • Monetization: donations/subscriptions/paid content (as available), creator milestones.

Phased rollout

  1. Phase 0 — Setup: analytics, attribution basics, website/landing pages, creator onboarding flow.
  2. Phase 1 — Supply: recruit 20–50 creators in 3–5 niches; publish baseline catalog content.
  3. Phase 2 — Demand: push discovery via SEO + social + communities; iterate on landing pages and top-of-funnel conversion.
  4. Phase 3 — Flywheel: programmatic SEO, creator referral program, partnerships, recurring events/contests.

Channel mix (initial)

  • Google / SEO: programmatic pages for niches/topics, plus evergreen editorial.
  • Social: creator-led distribution (short clips, threads, demos); platform account for highlights.
  • Communities: Discords, subreddits, forums, local groups; “value-first” participation.
  • Partnerships: newsletters/podcasts/influencers in niche learning spaces.
  • Email: onboarding + weekly digest; creator activation emails.
  • Paid (optional early): retargeting and small tests once conversion tracking is stable.

ChatGPT GPSs (GPT-powered systems)

  • Purpose: speed up marketing execution (research, drafts, test plans) while humans own truth and final decisions.
  • Where it helps most: niche research, message house drafts, SEO topic maps, social calendars, outreach/email drafts, experiment design.
  • Guardrails: don’t paste secrets or user data; don’t publish unverified claims.
  • Reference: see ChatGPT GPSs for reusable prompt templates and workflows.

Risks / guardrails

  • Thin content risk: prioritize quality niches and strong internal linking; avoid spammy programmatic pages.
  • Creator churn: focus on onboarding, fast “first win”, and distribution outcomes.
  • Trust: clear moderation, reporting, and consistent “what’s allowed” rules.
