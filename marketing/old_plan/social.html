Social plan (general sketch)

Principle

Creators are the distribution engine. The platform account supports: highlights, case studies, and events.

Channels (pick based on niches)

  • X / Threads: threads, short lessons, creator stories, product updates.
  • TikTok / Reels / Shorts: “one insight” clips + “watch me build/publish” demos.
  • YouTube: longer tutorials, creator interviews, niche playlists.
  • LinkedIn: professional learning niches, creator outcomes, platform milestones.
  • Discord: creator community + events; not just promotion.

Content formats

For creators (templates)

  • “I published X: here’s the outline”
  • “3 mistakes in X (and fixes)”
  • “Beginner roadmap for X”
  • “Before/after: student result” (where appropriate)

For the platform account

  • creator spotlights (1 per week)
  • curated lists (“Top 10 resources for X”) that link back to topic pages
  • feature releases framed as user outcomes (“publish in 2 minutes”)
  • events: contests, challenges, live sessions

Launch playbook (simple)

  1. Recruit 20–50 creators → help each publish 3–10 high-quality items.
  2. Give creators a “launch kit”: post templates, images, and a tracking link.
  3. Coordinate a launch window: daily drops + creator shout-outs.
  4. Collect and repost best-performing creator posts (permission-based).

Operating rhythm

  • Weekly: 1 creator spotlight, 1 curated list, 1 product update
  • Daily (lightweight): repost/highlight creator content
  • Monthly: event/contest + recap, “top creators of the month”

Success metrics

  • Clicks to landing pages; follow/start/save conversion
  • Creator participation rate (who posts about Kvasir)
  • Cost of time per activated creator (ops efficiency)
